Michael Jeffrey Jordan

The man who turned basketball into a global sport.

What Michael "Air" Jordan meant to basketball and his greatest accomplishments:

College

  • ACC Rookie Of the Year - 1982
  • Game-winning shot in the 1982 NCAA Championship game
  • ACC Player Of the Year - 1984
  • Consensus National College Player Of the Year - 1984
  • No. 23 retired by the UNC Tar Heels

National Basketball Association

  • 6 Time NBA Champion - 1991-1993, 1996-1998
  • 6 Time NBA Finals MVP
  • 5 Time NBA MVP - 1988, 1991, 1992, 1996, 1998
  • 14 Time NBA All-Star
  • 10 Time All NBA First team
  • NBA Defensive Player Of the Year - 1988
  • NBA Rookie Of the Year - 1985
  • 10 Time NBA scoring champion
  • 3 Time NBA steals leader

Legacy

The impact Michael "His Airness" Jordan had in the game of basketball is what defines his legacy. It was Jordan who truly made the game global with his electrifying playing style and world famous sneaker line, Air Jordan. In 2014, Jordan was named the first ever NBA player to become a billionaire, much due to the success of Air Jordan, which has helped build Nike in to one of the worlds leading sportswear brands.
Jordan has been inducted to the Naishmith Basketball Hall Of Fame for both his achievements in the NBA and his contributions to the 1992 Olympics USA Men's Basketball team, since dubbed "The Dream Team". His number 23 is retired both by the Chicago Bulls as well as the Miami Heat, even if Jordan never played a game for the Heat.

"I would never have called him the greatest player I'd ever seen if I didn't mean it. It's just God disguised as Michael Jordan."

NBA Legend Larry Bird, after Jordan scored 63 points on Bird's Celtics in the 1986 playoffs.
